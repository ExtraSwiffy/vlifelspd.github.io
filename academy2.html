LSPD Police Academy

Phase II – Traffic Stop Training

Overview

Phase II focuses on proper radio communication during traffic stops and applying classroom knowledge in real-world scenarios. This phase ensures cadets are fluent and confident when conducting traffic stops, Code 5 stops, and pursuits. All training is conducted in the open area behind the PD garage.

Training Focus

  • Physical Traffic Stops
  • Physical Code 5 Stops
  • Physical Vehicle Pursuits
  • Physical Foot Pursuits

Physical Traffic Stop

Use a personal civilian vehicle as the vehicle being stopped. Position your marked patrol vehicle behind it in the proper traffic stop position. Explain positioning and safety considerations to the cadets. Once demonstrated, have each cadet individually call out a traffic stop over the radio. After all cadets are confident and smooth, proceed to the next exercise.

  • Proper Traffic Stop Dispatch

Physical Code 5 Stop

Have each cadet, one at a time, properly dispatch a Code 5 stop. Train cadets on safely removing suspects one at a time and explain proper vehicle positioning using the diamond formation. Emphasize officer safety and clear communication throughout the stop.

  • Proper Code 5 Stop Dispatch

Physical Vehicle Pursuit

This exercise can become intense, especially with multiple cadets, so run it one cadet at a time. One cadet should drive the civilian vehicle, while the remaining cadets ride in PD-issued vehicles. The instructor should sit in the front passenger seat. Conduct the pursuit near the docks by the oil rig job site. Ensure proper pursuit dispatch is maintained. Rotate cadets through the driver position until each demonstrates confidence and control before moving on.

  • Proper Vehicle Pursuit Dispatch

Physical Foot Pursuit

While still at the docks, conduct a controlled foot pursuit scenario. Ensure cadets wait at least 15 seconds between taser deployments and verbally announce “Taser, Taser, Taser” prior to use. Cadets must provide clear suspect descriptions, direction of travel, and confirm that postols may be announced over the radio during foot pursuits.

  • Proper Foot Pursuit Dispatch
